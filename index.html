1st Order Integrated Rate Equation

Unit 3 | V. Imp
Step 1: Differential Rate Law

For reaction \(R \to P\), the rate is proportional to \([R]\):

\(\text{Rate} = -\frac{d[R]}{dt} = k[R]\)
Step 2: Rearrange and Integrate

Separate variables and integrate from \(t=0\) to \(t=t\):

\(\int \frac{d[R]}{[R]} = -\int k dt\)

\(\ln[R] = -kt + I\)

Step 3: Solve for Constant

At \(t=0\), \([R] = [R]_0\). Thus, \(I = \ln[R]_0\).

Step 4: Final Log Form

Substitute and convert to base-10 log:

\(k = \frac{2.303}{t} \log \frac{[R]_0}{[R]}\)
üí° Graph Tip: A plot of \(\log[R]\) vs \(t\) gives a straight line with slope \(= -k / 2.303\).

Half Life of 1st Order (\(t_{1/2}\))

Substitute \([R] = [R]_0 / 2\) and \(t = t_{1/2}\) in the integrated equation:

\(k = \frac{2.303}{t_{1/2}} \log \frac{[R]_0}{[R]_0/2} = \frac{2.303}{t_{1/2}} \log 2\)
\(t_{1/2} = \frac{0.693}{k}\)

üí° Notice that \(t_{1/2}\) does NOT depend on initial concentration for 1st order!

The Nernst Equation Proof

Unit 2 | Pure Logic
Step 1: Relation with Gibbs Energy
\(\Delta G = \Delta G^\circ + RT \ln Q\)
Step 2: Subbing Electrical Work

Since \(\Delta G = -nFE_{\text{cell}}\):

\(-nFE_{\text{cell}} = -nFE^\circ + RT \ln Q\)
Step 3: Simplify for 298K

Divide by \(-nF\) and use \(R=8.314, T=298\):

\(E_{\text{cell}} = E^\circ - \frac{0.059}{n} \log Q\)

Raoult's Law (Relative Lowering)

For a non-volatile solute:

\(P_1 = P_1^\circ x_1\)

Lowering of pressure \(\Delta P = P_1^\circ - P_1\):

\(\frac{P_1^\circ - P_1}{P_1^\circ} = x_2\)

üí° Where \(x_2\) is the mole fraction of the solute.
