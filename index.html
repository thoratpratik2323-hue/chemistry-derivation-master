1st Order Integrated Rate Equation

V. Imp | 5 Marks
Step 1: Introduction & Assumption

Consider a first-order reaction: \(R \to P\)

The rate of reaction is proportional to the concentration of reactant raised to the power 1.

\(\text{Rate} = -\frac{d[R]}{dt} = k[R]^1\)
Step 2: Separation of Variables

Rearranging the equation to separate concentration and time terms:

\(\frac{d[R]}{[R]} = -k dt\)
Step 3: Integration

Integrating both sides:

\(\int \frac{d[R]}{[R]} = -\int k dt\)

\(\ln[R] = -kt + I \quad \text{--- (Eq. 1)}\)

Where \(I\) is the constant of integration.

Step 4: Finding Constant \(I\)

At time \(t = 0\), concentration \([R] = [R]_0\) (Initial Concentration).

Substituting in Eq. 1: \(\ln[R]_0 = -k(0) + I \implies I = \ln[R]_0\)

Step 5: Final Expression

Substitute \(I\) back into Eq. 1:

\(\ln[R] = -kt + \ln[R]_0\)

\(kt = \ln[R]_0 - \ln[R] = \ln \frac{[R]_0}{[R]}\)

\(k = \frac{2.303}{t} \log \frac{[R]_0}{[R]}\)

Zero Order Integrated Rate Equation

3 Marks
Step 1: Rate Law

For \(R \to P\), Rate is independent of concentration:

\(-\frac{d[R]}{dt} = k[R]^0 = k\)
Step 2: Integration

\(d[R] = -k dt \implies [R] = -kt + I\)

At \(t=0, [R]=[R]_0 \implies I=[R]_0\)

Step 3: Final Form
\(k = \frac{[R]_0 - [R]}{t}\)

1st Order Integrated Rate Equation (Gas Phase)

Important
Step 1: Reaction Setup

Consider: \(A(g) \to B(g) + C(g)\)

Initial pressure at \(t=0\) is \(P_i\). At time \(t\), total pressure is \(P_t\).

Step 2: Partial Pressures

Let \(x\) be the decrease in pressure of A:

\(P_A = P_i - x, \quad P_B = x, \quad P_C = x\)

\(P_t = (P_i - x) + x + x = P_i + x \implies x = P_t - P_i\)

Step 3: Final Substitution

\(P_A = P_i - (P_t - P_i) = 2P_i - P_t\)

\(k = \frac{2.303}{t} \log \frac{P_i}{2P_i - P_t}\)

Derivation of Nernst Equation

Step 1: Thermodynamic Relation

From thermodynamics, Gibbs free energy change is:

\(\Delta G = \Delta G^\circ + RT \ln Q\)

Where \(Q\) is the reaction quotient.

Step 2: Conversion to Cell Potential

We know \(\Delta G = -nFE_{\text{cell}}\) and \(\Delta G^\circ = -nFE^\circ_{\text{cell}}\)

\(-nFE_{\text{cell}} = -nFE^\circ_{\text{cell}} + RT \ln Q\)
Step 3: Final Form (298K)
\(E_{\text{cell}} = E^\circ_{\text{cell}} - \frac{0.059}{n} \log Q\)

Relation between \(E^\circ_{\text{cell}}\) and \(K_c\)

Step 1: Equilibrium Condition

At equilibrium, \(E_{\text{cell}} = 0\) and \(Q = K_c\).

Step 2: Substitute in Nernst
\(0 = E^\circ_{\text{cell}} - \frac{2.303 RT}{nF} \log K_c\)
Step 3: Final Form (298K)
\(E^\circ_{\text{cell}} = \frac{0.059}{n} \log K_c\)

Also, \(\Delta G^\circ = -nFE^\circ_{\text{cell}} = -2.303 RT \log K_c\)

Elevation in Boiling Point (\(\Delta T_b\))

Relating molality (\(m\)) to molar mass of solute (\(M_2\)):

Step 1: Relation

\(\Delta T_b = K_b \cdot m\)

Step 2: Subbing Molality

Molality \(m = \frac{w_2 \times 1000}{M_2 \times w_1}\)

Step 3: Final Molar Mass Formula
\(M_2 = \frac{1000 \times K_b \times w_2}{\Delta T_b \times w_1}\)

Relative Lowering of Vapour Pressure

Derivation

\(P_1 = P_1^\circ x_1 = P_1^\circ (1 - x_2)\)

\(P_1^\circ - P_1 = P_1^\circ x_2\)

\(\frac{P_1^\circ - P_1}{P_1^\circ} = x_2 = \frac{n_2}{n_1 + n_2}\)

Osmotic Pressure (\(\pi\))

Van't Hoff Equation

\(\pi \propto C\) and \(\pi \propto T\)

\(\pi = CRT = \frac{n_2}{V} RT\)

\(M_2 = \frac{w_2 RT}{\pi V}\)

Van't Hoff Factor (\(i\)) & Dissociation (\(\alpha\))

Step 1: The Setup

Consider dissociation: \(A_n \to nA\)

Initially: 1 mole. After dissociation: \((1-\alpha)\) and \(n\alpha\).

Step 2: Total Moles

Total moles = \((1-\alpha) + n\alpha = 1 + \alpha(n-1)\)

Step 3: Relation

\(i = \frac{\text{Observed}}{\text{Normal}} = \frac{1 + \alpha(n-1)}{1}\)

\(\alpha = \frac{i - 1}{n - 1}\)

Density of a Unit Cell

Legendary Chapter
Step 1: Mass of Unit Cell

Mass = \(Z \times m\) (Where \(Z\) is number of atoms, \(m\) is mass of single atom).

\(m = M / N_A\) (Molar mass / Avogadro number).

Step 2: Volume of Unit Cell

Volume \(V = a^3\) (For a cubic cell with edge length \(a\)).

Step 3: Density formula
\(d = \frac{Z \cdot M}{a^3 \cdot N_A}\)

Packing Efficiency (SCC)

In Simple Cubic, \(a = 2r\). Atoms touch along edge.

\(Z = 1\). Volume of atom = \(\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\).

Total volume = \(a^3 = 8r^3\).

\(\text{P.E.} = \frac{\pi}{6} = 52.4\%\)

Packing Efficiency (BCC)

Body diagonal \(c = \sqrt{3}a = 4r \implies a = \frac{4r}{\sqrt{3}}\)

\(Z = 2\). Volume of atoms = \(2 \times \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\).

\(\text{P.E.} = \frac{\sqrt{3}\pi}{8} = 68\%\)

Packing Efficiency (FCC / CCP)

Face diagonal \(b = \sqrt{2}a = 4r \implies a = 2\sqrt{2}r\)

\(Z = 4\). Volume of atoms = \(4 \times \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\).

\(\text{P.E.} = \frac{\pi}{3\sqrt{2}} = 74\%\)

Freundlich Adsorption Isotherm

Relating mass of gas adsorbed (\(x\)) per mass of adsorbent (\(m\)) to pressure (\(P\)):

\(\frac{x}{m} = k P^{1/n} \quad (n > 1)\)

Taking log on both sides:

\(\log \frac{x}{m} = \log k + \frac{1}{n} \log P\)

The Arrhenius Equation

Standard Form
\(k = A e^{-E_a/RT}\)
Log Form for two temperatures
\(\log \frac{k_2}{k_1} = \frac{E_a}{2.303R} \left[ \frac{T_2 - T_1}{T_1T_2} \right]\)
